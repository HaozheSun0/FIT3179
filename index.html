Chart 1: Life Expectancy Worldwide

As can be seen from Chart 1, the distribution of life expectancy around the world is extremely uneven. Life expectancy at birth is generally higher in Europe than in other regions, while life expectancy at birth is lowest in sub-Saharan Africa. Sub-Saharan African countries consistently rank among the lowest-income countries in terms of GDP per capita. Therefore, there is considerable room for improvement in life expectancy in sub-Saharan countries. The world's two superpowers, the United States and China, are not above many European countries.
